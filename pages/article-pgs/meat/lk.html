Lechon Kawali

Lechon Kawali is another crispy pork belly dish, similar to Crispy Pata but using a slab of pork belly (liempo). The meat is first boiled in a seasoned liquid until tender, then air-dried, and finally deep-fried in a wok (kawali) until the skin becomes blistered and unbelievably crisp. It is a faster alternative to traditional Lechon.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg slab of pork belly (liempo)
  • 4 cups water (or enough to cover the pork)
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • 3 dried bay leaves
  • Cooking oil (for deep frying)
  • For Dipping Sauce: Lechon sauce (or a blend of vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic)

Cooking Procedure

  1. Boil the Pork.
    In a large pot, place the pork belly. Add water, onion, garlic, salt, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil.
  2. Tenderize.
    Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork belly is fork tender.
  3. Drain and Dry.
    Remove the pork belly from the broth and place it on a wire rack. Pat the skin completely dry with paper towels.
  4. Air-Dry and Chill.
    Rub a little salt on the skin. Air-dry and chill the pork belly uncovered in the refrigerator for at least 3 hours or, ideally, overnight. This step is essential for maximum crispiness.
  5. Deep Fry (First Fry).
    Heat enough oil in a deep pan or kawali over medium heat. Fry the pork belly (skin-side down first) for about 10-15 minutes until the skin is lightly browned and hard. Remove and drain.
  6. Deep Fry (Second Fry/High Heat).
    Increase the heat to high. Once the oil is smoking hot, carefully return the pork belly to the oil. Fry quickly for 3 to 5 minutes, turning occasionally, until the skin blisters and achieves that characteristic bubbly crispiness.
  7. Serve
    Remove from the oil, drain excess fat, and let it rest briefly. Chop the crispy pork belly into serving pieces and serve hot with Lechon sauce.
