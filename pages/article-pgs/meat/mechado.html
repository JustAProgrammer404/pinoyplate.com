Tatak Pinoy

Mechado

Mechado is another popular Filipino beef stew, often confused with Kaldereta and Afritada. Its key difference lies in the traditional use of larded beef (a strip of pork fat inserted into a thick cut of beef), which is simmered in a savory, sour, tomato-based sauce flavored with lemon or calamansi, soy sauce, and bay leaves. It typically includes potatoes and carrots.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into cubes (or mechado cut)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice (or calamansi juice)
  • 2 large potatoes, quartered
  • 2 large carrots, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 7 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 5 dried bay leaves
  • 2 1/2 cups water or beef broth
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • Salt to taste

Cooking Procedure

  1. Marinate the Beef.
    In a large bowl, combine the beef, soy sauce, lemon/calamansi juice, and pepper. Mix well. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour or overnight.
  2. Fry the Vegetables.
    In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Fry the potatoes and carrots until the edges are lightly browned. Set aside.
  3. Sear the Beef.
    In the same pot, increase the heat to high and sear the marinated beef until browned on all sides (reserve the marinade). Remove the beef and set aside.
  4. Sauté Aromatics.
    Lower the heat to medium. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  5. Add Liquids.
    Pour in the reserved marinade and the tomato sauce. Let it simmer for 2 minutes. Add the water/broth and bay leaves.
  6. Braise the Beef.
    Return the seared beef to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then cover and let it simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is fork tender.
  7. Finish the Stew.
    Add back the fried potatoes and carrots. Add the bell peppers (optional) and simmer until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Serve
    Remove the bay leaves. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt. Serve hot with steamed rice.
