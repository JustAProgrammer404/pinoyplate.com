Tatak Pinoy

Sisig

Sisig is a highly popular Filipino dish, often served on a sizzling plate. It is traditionally made from parts of a pig's head (ears, snout, face/cheek) and chicken liver, which are boiled, grilled, and then finely chopped. The chopped meat is seasoned with lime juice (calamansi), chopped onions, and chili peppers, and often topped with mayonnaise or a raw egg.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ lbs. Pork belly (or pig face/maskara)
  • 1/2 cup Lady's Choice Mayonnaise
  • 2 pcs. Onion, chopped
  • 4 pcs. Thai chili pepper, chopped (or siling labuyo)
  • 2 pcs. Long green pepper (siling haba), chopped
  • 2 tablespoons Soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon Ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup Margarine
  • 2 cups Cooking oil
  • Knorr SavorRich Pork (or seasoning of choice)
  • Calamansi juice (for serving)

Cooking Procedure

  1. Prepare the Pork.
    Boil the pork belly (or pig parts) until very tender. If using pork belly, boil for about 1 hour. Drain the pork and pat it completely dry.
  2. Fry the Pork.
    Heat the cooking oil in a pan. Fry the boiled pork belly until the skin becomes golden brown and crispy. Remove from oil, drain excess fat, and let it cool slightly.
  3. Chop the Pork.
    Finely chop the crispy pork belly into small, sisig-sized pieces. Set aside.
  4. Sauté the Aromatics.
    Heat half of the margarine in a clean pan. Sauté half of the chopped onions until softened.
  5. Combine and Season.
    Add the chopped pork to the pan. Sauté for about 30 seconds. Add the soy sauce, Knorr SavorRich (or seasoning), chili peppers, and ground black pepper. Cook for 1 minute.
  6. Add Creaminess.
    Turn off the heat. Stir in the mayonnaise. Add the remaining chopped onion. Mix well.
  7. Sizzle and Serve.
    Heat a metal plate (sizzling plate) on a stovetop until smoking hot. Melt the remaining margarine on the plate. Transfer the sisig mixture onto the hot plate. Crack a raw egg on top (optional). Serve immediately with rice and a side of calamansi wedges.
