Tatak Pinoy

Menudo

Menudo is a hearty Filipino stew, typically made with cubed pork (or occasionally beef) and pork liver, simmered in a rich tomato-based sauce. It is characterized by its colorful mix of diced potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and sometimes raisins and sliced hotdogs, making it a staple for special occasions and daily meals.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs boneless pork loin, cubed (menudo cut)
  • 1/4 lb pig liver, cubed
  • 1 (8 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 1 ½ cups water (or broth)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 3 pcs calamansi (or 1 tbsp lemon juice)
  • 1 medium onion, minced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium potato, diced
  • 1 medium carrot, cubed
  • 3 pcs hotdogs, sliced diagonally
  • 4 pcs dried bay leaves (laurel)
  • 1/2 cup raisins (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon granulated white sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil

Cooking Procedure

  1. Marinate the Beef.
    In a bowl, combine the pork cubes with soy sauce and calamansi juice. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Fry the Vegetables.
    In a wok or deep pot, heat cooking oil. Fry the carrots and potatoes until lightly browned and set aside. Toss in the sliced hotdogs and bell pepper, stir and set aside as well.
  3. Sauté Aromatics.
    In the same pot, sauté the minced garlic and diced onion until fragrant.
  4. Cook the Pork.
    Add the marinated pork (reserve the marinade) and cook until the pork changes color. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Braise the Pork.
    Pour the reserved marinade, tomato sauce, and water/broth into the pot. Add the bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and let the pork simmer for 30–40 minutes or until tender.
  6. Add Liver and Seasoning.
    Add the cubed liver and sugar. Simmer for about 5 minutes.
  7. Finish the Stew.
    Stir in the previously fried potatoes, carrots, hotdogs, bell peppers, and raisins (if using). Cook for another 3–5 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  8. Serve
    Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve hot with steamed rice.
