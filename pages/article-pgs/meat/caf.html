Tatak Pinoy

Chicken Afritada

Afritada is a classic Filipino chicken stew (often pork or beef) characterized by its savory tomato-based sauce. The chicken is braised with potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and often green peas. It is similar to Menudo and Kaldereta but generally lighter, relying on tomato sauce and sometimes fish sauce (patis) for its rich, umami flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 1/2 tablespoon fish sauce (patis)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 medium potatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1 small carrot, cut into wedges
  • 1 piece chicken broth cube (optional)
  • 1 cup water or chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup green peas (canned or frozen)
  • 1 large green or red bell pepper, cut into wedges
  • Cooking oil
  • Salt and sugar, to taste

Cooking Procedure

  1. Sauté the Aromatics.
    In a deep pot, heat cooking oil. Sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until aromatic.
  2. Sear the Chicken.
    Stir in the chicken pieces. Sauté until the chicken changes color and is lightly browned.
  3. Season.
    Add ground black pepper and fish sauce. Stir to blend and simmer for about 3 minutes to allow the fish sauce aroma to mellow.
  4. Add Tomato Base.
    Stir in the tomato paste and chicken broth cube (if using). Stir to combine.
  5. Braise.
    Add the water or chicken broth. Cover and let it simmer for 15 to 20 minutes over medium heat.
  6. Add Root Vegetables.
    After simmering, add the potatoes, carrots, and tomato sauce. Stir to combine. Cover again and simmer for another 15 minutes or until the potatoes and carrots are tender.
  7. Finish the Dish.
    Stir in the green peas and bell pepper wedges. Taste the sauce, adding a little sugar or salt if needed. Cook for another 3 to 5 minutes until the sauce has thickened slightly and the vegetables are cooked but still firm.
  8. Serve.
    Remove from heat and serve hot with steamed rice.
