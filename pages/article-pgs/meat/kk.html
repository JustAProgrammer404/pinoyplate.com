Tatak Pinoy

Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a Filipino peanut stew made with oxtail, beef, or tripe simmered until tender and coated in a thick, creamy peanut sauce enriched with annatto and ground toasted rice. It is served with vegetables like eggplant, sitaw, and pechay, and eaten with bagoong alamang for salty contrast.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg oxtail (or mix of oxtail + beef shank)
  • Water for boiling
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • Salt, to taste
  • ½ cup peanut butter (or 1 cup ground roasted peanuts)
  • 1 tbsp annatto powder or ¼ cup annatto water
  • 2 tbsp ground toasted rice (or 1 tbsp cornstarch slurry)
  • 2 eggplants, sliced lengthwise
  • 1 bundle sitaw (string beans), cut into 2–3 inch pieces
  • 1 bunch pechay or bok choy
  • Banana heart (optional), sliced
  • Cooking oil

Cooking Procedure

  1. Clean the meat.
    Rinse the oxtail and beef pieces under running water. Remove impurities and drain well.
  2. Boil and tenderize the meat.
    Place the meat in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil over high heat, skim off scum, then add onion and garlic. Lower heat and simmer 2–3 hours (or pressure cook 35–45 minutes) until very tender.
  3. Prepare the vegetables.
    Slice the eggplants, cut the sitaw, and wash the pechay. Set them aside separately.
  4. Blanch the vegetables.
    In boiling water, blanch the sitaw for 1 minute, remove, then blanch eggplants and pechay. Arrange them on a tray for later use.
  5. Start the peanut sauce.
    In a large pan, heat oil over medium heat. Sauté minced garlic, then add annatto powder or annatto water and mix until the color is released.
  6. Add the peanut component.
    Add peanut butter (or ground peanuts) to the pan. Stir until smooth, then ladle in 2–3 cups of hot broth from the meat pot. Mix until a thick sauce forms.
  7. Thicken with toasted rice.
    Add ground toasted rice (or cornstarch slurry) to adjust the thickness. Stir continuously to avoid lumps.
  8. Combine meat and sauce.
    Transfer the tender meat pieces into the peanut sauce. Add more broth if necessary to get a creamy consistency.
  9. Simmer the stew.
    Cook over low heat for 10–15 minutes so the meat absorbs the peanut flavor.
  10. Add the vegetables.
    Place the sitaw, eggplant, and pechay on top of the stew. Cover and let them heat for 2–3 minutes without overcooking.
  11. Season lightly.
    Add salt only if needed. Kare-Kare is intentionally mild because bagoong provides the main seasoning.
  12. Serve.
    Transfer the stew to a serving bowl, keeping vegetables arranged neatly on top. Serve hot with bagoong alamang on the side.
