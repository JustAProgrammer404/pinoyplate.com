Kaldereta

Kaldereta (or Caldereta) is a rich and savory Filipino beef (or goat) stew. It is distinguished from other tomato-based stews like Afritada and Menudo by its bolder, spicier flavor, which comes from ingredients like liver spread, chili flakes, and sometimes cheese or peanut butter. The beef is simmered until fall-apart tender in a sauce of tomato sauce, potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and olives.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. beef chuck, sliced into cubes
  • 1 large potato, sliced into cubes
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 1 small green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 5 tablespoons liver spread
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 cup beef stock (or water and 1 beef cube)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (or siling labuyo)
  • 1/2 cup pitted green olives (optional)
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil

Cooking Procedure

  1. Marinate the Beef.
    In a bowl, combine the beef cubes with soy sauce and black pepper. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Fry the Vegetables.
    In a large pot, heat the cooking oil. Fry the potato and carrot cubes until the edges are lightly browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. Sauté Aromatics.
    In the same pot, sauté the chopped onion until translucent, then add the crushed garlic and cook until fragrant.
  4. Sear the Beef.
    Add the marinated beef cubes and sear until lightly browned on all sides.
  5. Braise the Beef.
    Pour in the tomato sauce, beef stock/water, and vinegar. Add the red pepper flakes and liver spread. Stir well until the liver spread is fully dissolved into the sauce. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  6. Simmer.
    Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is very tender. Stir occasionally.
  7. Add Vegetables.
    Once the beef is tender, add the fried potatoes, carrots, and the sliced bell peppers. Add the olives (if using).

  8. Finish the Dish
    Cover and continue to simmer for 5 to 10 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper.
  9. Serve
    Transfer the Kaldereta to a serving dish and serve hot with steamed white rice.
