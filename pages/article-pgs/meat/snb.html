Tatak Pinoy

Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang na Baboy is a beloved Filipino sour pork soup characterized by a tangy tamarind-based broth. Tender pork pieces are simmered with vegetables like kangkong, eggplant, and radish, creating a comforting, flavorful, and slightly sour dish perfect with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or shoulder, cut into cubes
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack tamarind soup base (or 1/2 cup fresh tamarind pulp)
  • 2 cups radish, sliced
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans (sitaw), cut into 3-inch pieces
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2–3 long chili peppers (optional)
  • Fish sauce or salt, to taste

Cooking Procedure

  1. Prepare the Pork.
    Rinse the pork cubes under running water and set aside.
  2. Boil the pork.
    In a large pot, combine pork, water, onion, and tomatoes. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  3. Skim the scum.
    As the water boils, skim off any impurities or foam from the surface for a clear broth.
  4. Simmer the pork.
    Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 45–60 minutes or until the pork is tender.
  5. Add the souring agent.
    Stir in the tamarind soup base or fresh tamarind pulp. Adjust to your preferred sourness.
  6. Add the vegetables in stages.
    Start with the radish and string beans; cook for 5–7 minutes. Then add the eggplant and chili peppers (if using); cook another 3–5 minutes.
  7. Season the soup.
    Add fish sauce or salt to taste. Stir gently to combine flavors.
  8. Add the leafy greens.
    Finally, add the kangkong and simmer for 1–2 minutes until just wilted.
  9. Check the taste.
    Adjust sourness or seasoning as desired.
  10. Serve.
    Transfer to a soup bowl and serve hot with steamed rice.
