Crispy Pata

Crispy Pata is a Filipino dish consisting of deep-fried whole pork leg (pata) that has been pre boiled and seasoned. The goal is to achieve extremely tender meat on the inside and incredibly crisp, crackling skin on the outside. It is usually served with a dipping sauce of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and chili peppers.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole pork leg (pata), cleaned (about 2–3 kg)
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 1 head garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves (laurel)
  • 1 tbsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp salt (plus more for rubbing)
  • Cooking oil (for deep frying)
  • For Dipping Sauce:Vinegar, soy sauce, minced garlic, chopped onion, chopped chili (siling labuyo)

Cooking Procedure

  1. Boil the Pata.
    In a large pot, place the whole pork leg and cover it with water. Add the onion, crushed garlic, bay leaves, peppercorns, salt, vinegar, and soy sauce.
  2. Tenderize.
    Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 2 to 3 hours, or until the pork leg is very tender. Alternatively, use a pressure cooker for about 45 minutes.
  3. Drain and Dry.
    Remove the pork leg from the liquid and drain it well. Pat the skin completely dry with paper towels.
  4. Season the Skin.
    Rub the entire skin of the pork leg generously with salt.
  5. Chill.
    Refrigerate the pork leg uncovered for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. Chilling and drying the skin helps achieve the ultimate crispiness.
  6. Deep Fry.
    In a deep cooking pot or wok, heat enough cooking oil for deep frying over medium-high heat. Carefully lower the pork leg into the hot oil.
  7. Achieve Crispiness.
    Fry the pork leg for 15 to 20 minutes, turning it constantly, until the skin is bubbly, golden brown, and perfectly crisp.
  8. Rest and Serve.
    Remove the Crispy Pata from the oil and drain excess grease. Let it rest for 5-10 minutes before chopping into serving pieces. Serve immediately with the prepared dipping sauce.
