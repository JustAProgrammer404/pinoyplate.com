Chicken Adobo

Chicken Adobo is a classic Filipino braised dish simmered in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns until tender, flavorful, and coated in a savory-tangy glaze. It is one of the easiest and most iconic Filipino dishes.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken thigh/drumstick (bone-in, skin-on)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 7 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt (optional)

Cooking Procedure

  1. Prepare the chicken.
    Rinse the chicken pieces under running water, pat dry with paper towels, and place them in a large bowl.
  2. Make the marinade.
    Pour in the soy sauce and vinegar. Add the crushed garlic cloves, bay leaves, and whole peppercorns. Mix the chicken thoroughly so all pieces are coated.
  3. Marinate.
    Cover the bowl and let it sit for at least 1 hour. For deeper flavor, refrigerate it for 3–12 hours, turning the pieces once halfway if possible.
  4. Separate chicken from marinade.
    After marinating, lift the chicken pieces out and set them on a plate. Keep all the liquid marinade — you will use it for braising.
  5. Heat oil.
    Place a pot or deep pan over medium heat. Add cooking oil and allow it to heat completely.
  6. Brown the chicken pieces.
    Place the chicken skin-side down in the hot oil. Sear each piece for 3–4 minutes per side, or until the skin becomes lightly browned. This step develops deeper flavor.
  7. Add the marinade back into the pot.
    Pour the reserved marinade into the pan, letting the soy-vinegar mixture cover the chicken partially.
  8. Add water.
    Add 1 cup of water to the pot to ensure the chicken has enough liquid to simmer in.
  9. Bring to a boil.
    Turn the heat to high and allow the mixture to boil uncovered for 2–3 minutes.
  10. Simmer the chicken.
    Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and allow the chicken to cook for 30–40 minutes. Turn the pieces halfway through so both sides cook evenly.
  11. Adjust seasoning.
    Taste the sauce. If you want a touch of sweetness, add 1 tablespoon of brown sugar. Add salt only if necessary (soy sauce is usually salty enough).
  12. Reduce the sauce.
    Remove the lid and continue cooking uncovered over medium heat until the sauce thickens to a glossy consistency and coats the chicken pieces. This may take another 8–15 minutes depending on heat level.
  13. Finish.
    Turn off the heat. Let the chicken sit in the sauce for 5 minutes so the flavors settle.
  14. Serve.
    Transfer the chicken and the thickened sauce to a serving bowl. Serve hot with freshly cooked steamed rice.
