Tatak Pinoy

Arroz Caldo

Chicken arroz caldo is a type of Filipino rice porridge with chicken slices. It is best served when topped with hard boiled eggs, some chopped scallions, and toasted or roasted garlic. I personally like to squeeze a piece of calamansi or lime over the toppings. It makes the dish taste fresher and delicious.

Ingredients

  • Bihon noodles (rice noodles)
  • Ground pork
  • Shrimp stock
  • Annatto (atsuete)
  • Garlic & onion
  • Cornstarch
  • Tinapa flakes
  • Hard-boiled eggs
  • Cooked shrimp
  • Chicharrón
  • Spring onions
  • Fish sauce

Cooking Procedure

  1. Prepare the chicken (cut into pieces, bone-in preferred for flavor).
  2. Mince garlic and onions; julienne ginger. In a pot, heat oil, sauté garlic until golden, remove some for garnish, then sauté onion and ginger.
  3. Add chicken pieces, sauté until lightly browned.
  4. Add rice (sometimes a mix of jasmine + glutinous rice) and stir for 30 seconds.
  5. Pour in water (or chicken broth) and bring to boil, then reduce heat and simmer until rice breaks down and becomes thick, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Season with fish sauce / salt / pepper.
  6. Once the porridge is thick and chicken is cooked/tender, transfer to bowls. Top with toasted garlic bits, chopped green onions, hard-boiled egg halves, and add a squeeze of calamansi or lemon.
