Tatak Pinoy

Laing

A creamy, spicy vegetable dish from the Bicol Region made with dried taro (gabi) leaves cooked in coconut milk and chili, often with pork or shrimp and shrimp paste.

Ingredients

  • 500 g dried taro (gabi) leaves
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 100 g shrimp or dried fish (optional)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2–3 chili peppers (labuyo)
  • 1–2 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil

Cooking Procedure

  1. Sauté garlic, onion, ginger and optional pork or shrimp in oil.
  2. Add coconut milk (or a mixture of coconut milk + cream) and shrimp paste/bagoong.
  3. Add the dried taro leaves. Do not stir immediately—let the leaves absorb the coconut milk until they sink.
  4. Once the leaves soften and soak up the liquid, gently stir, then continue cooking until the coconut milk reduces and the sauce thickens.
  5. Add chilis (labuyo) or pepper to taste, adjust seasoning, serve hot with steamed rice.
