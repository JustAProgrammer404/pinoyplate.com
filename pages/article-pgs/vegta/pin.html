Tatak Pinoy

Pinakbet

A hearty Filipino vegetable stew of mixed local veggies (e.g., squash, eggplant, okra, bitter melon, string beans) flavored with shrimp paste (bagoong). Originates from the Ilocos Region but widely cooked in Tagalog regions too.

Ingredients

  • 1 small squash, cubed
  • 1 small eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2‑inch pieces
  • 1 bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced
  • 1–2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 100 g pork belly (optional)
  • 1–2 cups water
  • Cooking oil

Cooking Procedure

  1. Heat oil; sauté onion, garlic, and tomatoes.
  2. Add pork belly (optional) and sauté until lightly browned.
  3. Add shrimp paste/bagoong; stir.
  4. Add water, bring to boil, then add harder vegetables first (e.g., squash).
  5. Then add remaining vegetables (eggplant, okra, bitter melon, string beans) and cook until tender‑crisp.
  6. Season with salt and pepper; serve hot.
